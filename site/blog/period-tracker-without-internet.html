Phone-only cycle logs

Why a period tracker may not need internet access

Updated June 28, 2026

If all you want is a clear record of your period, symptoms, mood, and notes, the app does not need to send that information across the internet. It can simply save your log on the device you are using.

Internet access is a door

Internet access is not automatically bad. It can power sync, backups, support, and shared accounts. But it is still a door. If an app does not need that door for the main job, leaving it closed makes the privacy promise easier to understand.

Local phone-only is easier to explain

A local phone-only period tracker has a shorter story: your cycle notes are saved on your phone or browser, not sent to a company account for everyday tracking. You can still delete individual entries or clear your local log when you want.

What open-cycle proves in the app

The Android build does not request the internet permission. The website and app avoid ads, hidden analytics, and required cloud sync. That is the practical meaning of privacy-first here: fewer background systems, fewer guesses, and fewer places your period data can go by default.

What this does not solve

A local app cannot protect against every device-level risk. If someone has access to your unlocked phone, shared browser profile, or device backups, local data may still be visible there. Local-only is not magic. It is a smaller default path.
