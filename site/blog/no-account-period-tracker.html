No-account period tracking

A period tracker should not need your email to be useful

Updated June 28, 2026

Some people want cycle tracking without creating another account. That is reasonable. A period tracker can record dates, flow, symptoms, moods, and notes without asking who you are.

Why accounts change the feeling of an app

An account can be useful for sync, reminders, or sharing across devices. It can also create a new place where sensitive data may be stored, copied, requested, breached, sold, or used in ways you did not expect. For basic cycle logging, an account should not be the price of entry.

What to look for

Look for plain answers. Does the app work before you sign in? Does it say whether cycle notes leave your phone? Does it use ads or hidden analytics? Can you delete entries? Can you read the privacy policy without needing a lawyer?

How open-cycle handles it

open-cycle is a no-account period tracker for everyday logging. Your entries stay in local storage on your phone or browser. There are no ads, no ad SDKs, no hidden analytics, and no required cloud sync for core tracking.

When an account might still make sense

Some people may want extra features, like reminders, exports, or richer pattern views. That is why open-cycle links to Local Cycle as the optional one-time paid feature path. The free tracker stays simple.
